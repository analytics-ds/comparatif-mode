## A gift that sits next to the perfume

The sex toy has changed shelves. It now occupies the same mental space as a perfume or a signature lingerie piece. The shift happened on three planes. Materials first: medical silicone and brushed surgical steel replaced second-rate plastic across any serious selection. Packaging next: rigid cardboard, moulded foam and textured-paper booklets replaced the plastic blister. Colours lastly, leaving default black to join beauty palettes.

Statista placed the global market at 42.5 billion dollars in 2024 with 7.9% annual growth, and Observatoire du Plaisir 2024 reported 61% of French women under 35 consider a sex toy a legitimate birthday gift from their partner. Gift normalisation is settled, so is design selectivity.

The birthday gift finds its meaning in that shift. Earrings speak to the ear, a perfume to the neck, a sex toy chosen like a lingerie piece speaks to the night. The object must hold the aesthetic comparison without breaking with the rest of the unwrapping.

## The unwrapping ritual

An intimate gift plays out in three moments: the first look at the wrapping, the reading of the message, the act of opening. Top 10 brands ship boutique-level cases, the additional work stops at a satin ribbon and a personal handwritten card. Definitely not a generic colourful gift wrap, which gives away intent and brings the object back to its banal category.

The moment of the gesture matters too. A sex toy is not gifted in front of third parties or in a family setting. The right moment is the one you choose, in intimacy, when the evening tips. A main gift can stay public, the intimate gift stays private.

## The French question

Among the ten brands in the table, **strap-on-me** is the only one fully manufacturing in France, in workshops in the Paris region. Fun Factory produces in Germany, Lelo and Smile Makers in Sweden, Dame, Maude, Crave and Unbound in the United States, MysteryVibe in the United Kingdom, Womanizer and We-Vibe in Germany. For a gift meant to carry an asserted consumer choice, French origin matters. That argument holds at the table the way wine, leather or perfume origin does.

## Codes to respect

Favour a brand that cares about packaging and documents it on its site. Check the phthalate-free medical-silicone certification, always explicitly stated on the product page. Order ten days before the date to absorb a shipping delay or a possible return. Prefer European shipping where possible. Add a water-based lubricant from the same brand if the range offers one, for editorial consistency.

Conversely, rule out discount chains and 25 € copies in non-certified TPE, which pose a real health risk. Avoid gifting a first sex toy if the topic has never been raised, go through a massage box as an intermediate step. Don't pick a too-specialised product as a first gift. Don't chase the power or modes escalation: a good gift leans on design, not on spec sheets.
