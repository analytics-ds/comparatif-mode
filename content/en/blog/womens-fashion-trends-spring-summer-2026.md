## Quick take

1. The **Mocha Mousse and sage green palette** dominates spring-summer 2026 collections
2. **Oversized cuts** continue their progression, but the fitted dress returns on couture runways
3. **Natural fabrics** (linen, organic cotton, silk) overtake polyester
4. **Stacked accessories** (piercings, bracelets, fine necklaces) replace the single statement piece
5. The **capsule wardrobe** remains the dominant philosophy: fewer pieces, higher quality

In summary: spring-summer 2026 consolidates the return to natural fabrics and fluid oversized cuts, with a warm palette oriented toward terracotta and vegetal green. Accessorizing happens through stacking small pieces rather than a single statement.

## Spring-summer 2026 key colors

### Mocha Mousse, Pantone color of the year 2026

Mocha Mousse is the 2026 Pantone color of the year. This warm brown with terracotta nuances establishes itself as the signature color of the season. It is found on blazers, flowing pants, linen dresses, leather bags, and shoes.

French brands (Sessun, Sézane, A.P.C.) have widely integrated this color into their spring-summer 2026 collections. Mocha Mousse naturally pairs with off-white, deep black, and sage green.

### Sage green and sky blue

Sage green, soft and mineral, continues its progression started in 2024. It progressively replaces olive green on jackets, shirts, and accessories. Sky blue appears on oversized shirts and light pants, fresher than classic navy blue.

### The complete palette

- **Mocha Mousse** (warm terracotta brown): 2026 signature color
- **Sage green**: nature, mineral
- **Sand and warm beige**: functional neutrals
- **Off-white and ecru**: alternative to pure white
- **Black**: always present in accessories
- **Navy blue**: timeless basic

## Cuts dominating the runways

Oversized cuts continue their progression in accessible ready-to-wear (Sézane, Sessun, A.P.C.). Wide blazers worn over straight jeans, loose shirts tucked into tailored pants, and flowing A-line dresses form the typical spring-summer 2026 silhouette.

In contrast, couture runways (Dior, Saint Laurent, Balenciaga) bring back the midi fitted dress, defined at the waist. This trend will reach mid-tier ready-to-wear (Claudie Pierlot, Maje) from summer 2026.

## Season fabrics

The return to natural materials is the structural trend of spring-summer 2026:

- **Unbleached natural linen**: shirts, dresses, flowing pants
- **Organic cotton**: t-shirts, blouses, casual dresses
- **Silk and satin**: blouses, evening dresses
- **Viscose and tencel**: flowing dresses, midi skirts
- **Fine wools**: light knits, cardigans

Polyester and acrylic recede in mid-premium ready-to-wear, driven by growing demand for durable and biodegradable materials.

## Key accessories

### Stacked ear jewelry

The curated ear trend continues in 2026: stacking multiple piercings on the lobe, styled helix in fine gold, conch with structuring hoop, inter-piercing chains. Nebuleuse Bijoux, Mejuri, and Maria Tash dominate this segment.

### Structured leather bags

After several seasons dominated by soft or drawstring bags, structured full-grain leather bags return: Polène Numéro Un, Coach Tabby, Longchamp Roseau. The trend is toward naturally patinated leather, geometric shapes, and medium volumes.

### Scarves and belts

The silk square tied at the neck or as a bandana returns. Wide belts with marked buckles structure oversized silhouettes by defining the waist.
