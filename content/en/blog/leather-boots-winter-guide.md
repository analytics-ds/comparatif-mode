## Quick take

1. Choose **full-grain leather** for durability
2. The **lining** (wool or leather) drives warmth and comfort
3. A **Vibram sole** ensures grip on wet ground or snow
4. **Regular care** doubles the boots' lifespan

## The leather: full-grain or split?

Full-grain leather keeps the outer skin layer: more resistant, ages well and develops a patina. Split leather, from deeper layers, is less durable and loses its look quickly.

## The lining: wool or leather

Wool lining offers superior warmth for harsh winters. Calf leather lining is more breathable, ideal for mild climates.

## The sole

A lugged rubber sole (Vibram, Dainite, Commando) ensures grip. Avoid leather soles that become slippery on wet ground.

## The cut

Mid-calf boots (Chelsea, high chukka, lace-up) are versatile. Thigh-high boots are more seasonal. For daily use, go for mid-calf height.
