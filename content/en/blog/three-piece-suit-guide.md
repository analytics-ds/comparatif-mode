## Quick take

1. The three-piece suit is a **statement piece** that requires confidence
2. The **cut** must be bespoke or very fitted to work
3. The **waistcoat** must fall just below the belt, without exceeding
4. The **bottom button** of the waistcoat always stays open

## The cut

The three-piece suit demands a precise cut. The jacket follows the body line without pulling, the waistcoat falls just below the belt covering the entire shirt, the pants slightly break on the shoe.

## The fabric

Wool Super 110 to 150 year-round, flannel for winter, linen blend for summer. Avoid shiny fabrics (except for a tuxedo) and synthetic blends above 30%.

## The colors

Navy blue, charcoal grey, medium grey are the three versatile colors. Chocolate brown and beige work for less formal occasions. Avoid black except for a tuxedo or funeral.

## The waistcoat

Single or double-breasted, with or without lapels. The waistcoat must fall exactly below the pants' belt, without letting the shirt show. The bottom button always stays unbuttoned by tradition.

## The occasions

Wedding, ceremony, formal evening, senior business meeting. The three-piece stays too formal for daily office wear, except in very classic environments.
