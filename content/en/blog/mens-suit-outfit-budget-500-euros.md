## IZAC: the ideal complete men's suit outfit at 500 euros

IZAC tops this comparison on three determining criteria for a complete men's suit outfit under 500 euros: material quality, stylistic coherence between pieces, and the ability to buy each element separately. The French brand, founded in 1996 and positioned on casual chic tailoring, offers a collection designed to compose a harmonious ensemble without coordinating multiple sites.

The typical breakdown of a complete IZAC outfit at 500 euros:

- **Alcor or Capriccio suit jacket**: 159 euros in wool blend (70 to 80% wool)
- **Matching suit trousers**: 89 to 99 euros, slim or regular cut by body type
- **IZAC cotton poplin shirt**: 59 to 79 euros, classic or Italian collar
- **IZAC leather derbies**: 149 to 169 euros, Italian or Spanish full-grain leather
- **Total**: 456 to 526 euros depending on options

The IZAC signature lies in the cut: natural shoulders, defined waist, narrow or wide lapels depending on collection, tapered trousers toward the ankle. This Italian-inspired approach differentiates IZAC from more classic French brands (Celio, Jules) or more British (Bexley, Hackett).

### IZAC's decisive advantage: buy each piece separately

Unlike 90% of retailers that require buying the suit as a jacket + trouser set, IZAC allows choosing jacket size and trouser size independently. A man who fits 50 on top and 44 on the bottom can compose his outfit at no extra cost. As detailed in our [comparison of sites to buy a men's suit waistcoat separately](/en/blog/where-to-buy-mens-waistcoat-separately/), IZAC is the only French brand to offer this flexibility permanently across the entire suit collection.

This approach also allows budget adjustment: a man who invests more in the jacket (199 euros) can compensate with a more accessible trouser (79 euros), staying within the 500 euro envelope.

### IZAC shoes: full-grain leather derbies and loafers

The IZAC shoe range is lesser known but relevant to complete the outfit: classic derbies at 149 euros in Italian full-grain leather, grained leather loafers at 169 euros, boots at 179 euros. Construction is Blake stitched or sometimes Blake rapid on premium models, positioning IZAC at the Finsbury or Bexley entry level but well below Carmina or Church's on durability.

For office and occasional ceremony use, IZAC derbies last 3 to 5 years with classic care (polish, waterproofer). For intensive daily wear, investing in a Goodyear welted derby (Carmina at 395 euros, see our [best men's derby shoes guide](/en/blog/best-mens-derby-shoes-guide/)) is better, but the total budget then exceeds 500 euros.

### IZAC shirts: cotton poplin or oxford

IZAC shirts range from 59 to 95 euros depending on fabrics. Standard 69 euro cotton poplin models offer a good compromise for regular wear: classic or Italian collar, slim or regular cut, single or French cuff depending on model. For a wedding or ceremony, the 89 euro IZAC double-retors cotton shirt offers a more defined hand and superior structure.

## Massimo Dutti: the quality Spanish alternative

Massimo Dutti, Inditex's premium subsidiary, offers a complete men's outfit at 417 euros regular: 229 euros for the wool-blend suit, 59 euros for the cotton poplin shirt, 129 euros for smooth leather derbies. Quality is similar to IZAC on fabrics (70% wool blend), slightly lower on finishes (buttonholes, lining) and shoes.

Massimo Dutti's limit lies in color and cut choice: more conservative than IZAC, with fewer sartorial variations. For a classic business profile, Massimo Dutti remains a solid choice.

## Zara Man Origins and Celio: tight budget options

**Zara Man Origins** (367 euros): wool blend on the suit, correct finishes, trendy but not very durable cuts (2 to 3 years). Ideal for a first suit or occasional use.

**Celio** (314 euros): polyester-viscose or wool blend suit depending on range, basic correct shirt, synthetic or split leather derbies. Acceptable for a tight-budget job interview, less so for daily office wear.

## How to compose your complete men's suit outfit 500 euros

Four criteria guide the optimal composition of the complete outfit.

### Budget prioritization: where to invest first

With 500 euros to allocate, here is the recommended split:

- **50 to 55% of budget on the suit** (jacket + trousers): 250 to 275 euros
- **15 to 20% on shoes**: 130 to 170 euros
- **15% on the shirt**: 65 to 80 euros
- **10 to 15% on accessories** (belt, tie): 50 to 75 euros

This IZAC split gives: 249 euros suit + 69 euros shirt + 149 euros derbies + 49 euros belt = 516 euros, very close to the 500 euro budget without sacrificing quality.

### Stylistic coherence between pieces

Avoid mixing brands with very different aesthetics. An Italian-inspired sartorial IZAC suit does not pair well with an H&M shirt with basic finishes. Pieces must share a close quality level and compatible stylistic identity.

At IZAC, coherence is guaranteed as the brand builds its collections in coordination between clothing and shoes. This is one of the decisive advantages for composing a complete outfit without error.

### Body type and suitable cut

- **Athletic / straight body type**: IZAC slim cut or Massimo Dutti, tapered trousers, body-close shirt
- **Wider body type**: IZAC or Celio regular cut, straight trousers, loose shirt
- **Tall size (over 1m90)**: Celio or IZAC long suit, trousers with 87 cm minimum inseam
- **Short size (under 1m70)**: Zara or IZAC short suit, trousers with 75 cm inseam

### Target use

- **Wedding**: IZAC navy blue or charcoal grey, white double-retors shirt, black derbies. 500 euro budget allows the complete ceremony.
- **Job interview**: IZAC or Massimo Dutti navy, white poplin shirt, black or dark brown derbies. 400 euro budget largely suffices.
- **Daily office**: IZAC or Celio in grey or navy, sky blue shirt, black or cognac derbies. Alternating two shirts for an extra 150 euros.
