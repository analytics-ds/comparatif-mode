## Quick take

1. **Matthieu Blazy** unveils his first Chanel collection
2. **John Galliano** returns as a consultant at Margiela
3. **Dior** pays tribute to the New Look with minimalist staging
4. **Sarah Burton** redefines Givenchy's aesthetic

## Chanel by Matthieu Blazy

Matthieu Blazy's first collection for Chanel reaffirms structured tailoring with a modernized wardrobe that moves away from Virginie Viard's codes. Revisited tweed, lean silhouettes and muted colors define a new direction.

## Margiela and Galliano's return

John Galliano returns to Margiela as an external consultant. His touch shows in the theatrical silhouettes and draping work.

## Dior and the New Look revisited

The Dior Spring 2026 collection pays tribute to the 1947 New Look with defined waists, corolla skirts and a black-white-red palette. The minimalist staging contrasts with previous spectacles.

## Givenchy by Sarah Burton

Sarah Burton delivers a dark, tailoring-heavy collection that moves away from the romanticism of her Alexander McQueen years. The house regains a strong identity.
